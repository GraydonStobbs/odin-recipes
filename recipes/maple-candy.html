Maple Candy

How to Make Maple Candy

Maple leaf candy is an authentic sweet with just two ingredients: maple syrup and butter. It doesn't get much simpler—or sweeter—than that. Maple syrup is boiled, mixed with just a bit of butter, and then molded into beautiful leaf-shaped candies. This candy is a classic in New England and Canada—and for good reason—as it's a fun way to enjoy maple syrup.

Ingredients

  • Maple Syrup (Authentic)
  • Butter

Steps

  1. Pour the syrup into a medium, heavy-bottomed saucepan and place it over medium heat.
  2. Bring the syrup to a boil.
  3. Stir until the butter is fully melted and incorporated.
  4. Stir the candy vigorously with a wooden spoon until it thickens, lightens in color, and turns opaque, which should take about 3 to 5 minutes.
  5. Working quickly, spoon the candy into the candy molds.
  6. Allow them to cool, then pop them out of the molds
